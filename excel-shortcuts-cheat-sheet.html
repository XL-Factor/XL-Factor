Selecting Cells/Ranges

These shortcuts help quickly select cells or ranges in Excel.

Shortcut Use
Shift + Arrow Keys Extends your selection one cell at a time in the direction of the arrow key pressed, allowing precise control over what cells you highlight without using your mouse.
Ctrl + Space Selects the entire column of the currently active cell, perfect when you need to format or delete a whole column quickly without dragging your mouse.
Shift + Space Highlights the entire row of the active cell, making it easy to manipulate complete rows of data with subsequent commands like copy or delete.
Ctrl + A Selects all data in your current region when pressed once, or the entire worksheet when pressed twice, saving time on manual selection.
Ctrl + Shift + * (Asterisk) Quickly selects the current data region surrounding your active cell, ideal for working with tables or blocks of data without empty cells.
Shift + Click Extends your selection from the active cell to the cell you click on, creating a rectangular selection area without dragging the mouse.
Ctrl + Shift + Arrow Selects all cells from your current position to the last non-empty cell in the arrow's direction, great for large data ranges.
F8 (Extend Mode) Toggles extend selection mode where arrow keys expand your selection without holding Shift, useful for precise keyboard-only selection operations.
Ctrl + G → Enter Opens the Go To dialog and then immediately jumps back to your last selected cell, helping you return after scrolling away.
Alt + ; (Semicolon) Selects only visible cells within your current selection, bypassing hidden rows or columns when copying data in filtered lists.
